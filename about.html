About the City of Gweru

Discover the history, culture, and vision of Gweru, the heart of Zimbabwe's Midlands Province.

History

Gweru was founded in 1894 as a colonial outpost and grew into a key urban center due to its central location and fertile lands. Originally named "Gwelo," it was renamed Gweru after Zimbabwe's independence in 1980, reflecting its Shona heritage.

Geography

Located in the Midlands Province, Gweru sits at an altitude of 1,400 meters, offering a temperate climate. It serves as a gateway between Harare and Bulawayo, making it a vital economic and cultural hub.

Culture

Gweru is home to a diverse population, with a rich blend of Shona traditions and modern influences. The city hosts events like the Gweru Agricultural Show, celebrating its agricultural roots.

Vision

The City of Gweru aims to be a sustainable, inclusive, and prosperous urban center by 2030, focusing on infrastructure development, environmental conservation, and community empowerment.
